Selective articles that used NERDSS simulations

We kindly ask that you cite: Varga, M., Fu, Y., Loggia, S., Yogurtcu, O.N., & M.E. Johnson NERDSS: a nonequilibrium simulator for multibody self-assembly at the cellular scale. Biophysical Journal 118, P3026-P3040 (2020) in all publications that make use of NERDSS.

Below are a few selective articles that made use of NERDSS:

- Guo, Sikao, et al. "Structure of the HIV immature lattice allows for essential lattice remodeling within budded virions." elife 12 (2023): e84881.

- Qian, Yian, et al. "Temporal control by cofactors prevents kinetic trapping in retroviral Gag lattice assembly." Biophysical Journal 122.15 (2023): 3173-3190.

- Husar, Adam, et al. "Mcell4 with bionetgen: A Monte Carlo simulator of rule-based reaction-diffusion systems with python interface." PLOS Computational Biology 20.4 (2024): e1011800.

- Guo, Sikao, et al. "Structure of the HIV immature lattice allows for essential lattice remodeling within budded virions" eLife 12 (2023): e84881.

- Fu, Yiben, et al. "A continuum membrane model can predict curvature sensing by helix insertion." Soft Matter, 2021,17, 10649-10663 (2021)

- Guo, Sikao, et al. "Large self-assembled clathrin lattices spontaneously disassemble without sufficient adaptor proteins." Biophysical Journal 121.3 (2022): 331a.

- Fu, Yiben, et al. "A continuum membrane model can predict curvature sensing by helix insertion." Soft Matter 17.47 (2021): 10649-10663.

- Jiang, Anmin, et al. "Dynamin1 long-and short-tail isoforms exploit distinct recruitment and spatial patterns to form endocytic nanoclusters." Nature Communications 15.1 (2024): 4060.

- Johnson, M. E., et al. "The Roles of Space and Stochasticity in Computational Simulations of Cellular Biochemistry: Quantitative Analysis and Qualitative Insights." bioRxiv (2020): 2020-07.
